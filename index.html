Introduction

There are various type of horseback riding forms. In this document, we will explain the various types. The first three are English stle of riding while the last one is Western.

The main difference between English style and Western in that English style horses jump, while Western horses do not. Also, the riding equipment is a bit different. Within English style, Jumpers and Hunters are very similar except for the style of compeition. Drassage horses are different in that they don't jump typically.

Jumpers

The style of competition is not based on looks but their performance in keeping up all the jumps the most quickly. Jumpers is what you usually see in the olympics and World Cup. It is also know as equestrian showjumping.

There are various types of horses for this, but the most common are warm bloods. The best ones are bred in Europe, so riders in the US often go to Europe to try horses and ship them back to the US.

Hunters

This style is similar to jumpers, but the horse and rider are judged by their looks and quality of their jumping. The jumps are usually easier to keep up, and there isn't a time constraint.

These horses usually have a calmer temperment. Often times riders want them to move fancy and jump cute because that helps them get a better score.

Drassage

This style is usally judged on making the horse move and transition well intro trotting and cantering. The riders have exceptional control over the movement of the horse.

These horses are usally a certain type that is bred for this. You will often see a black horse with a rider wearing a big black tophat.

Western

This style various and is broken up into different forms

  • Team Penning
  • Cutting
  • Reining
  • Roping
  • Speed Games

These horses are usally built differently and really good at shifting and cutting quickly rather than for jumping.
